Kevin has been obsessed with stories for as long as I can remember. Not casually interested. Not "that would be nice someday." Obsessed.

His first unofficial debut happened when he was in the 9th grade. He made a short film that managed to shake an entire school. Think about that for a second. Most people spend their school years trying not to be noticed. Kevin made something that people couldn't stop talking about.

And he has been chasing that feeling ever since.

For more than eight years, he has dedicated himself to writing, directing, learning, failing, improving, rewriting, and starting over. While most people see a finished story, they don't see the years behind it. They don't see the late nights, the abandoned drafts, the notebooks full of ideas, or the countless hours spent trying to make something better than it was the day before.

Kevin has written more stories than most people realize. Not just pages. Books. Entire worlds. Characters, conversations, scenes, and ideas that only existed because he cared enough to bring them to life.

What makes this even more frustrating is that he is one of the first people to support someone else's dream. If a person wants to learn about filmmaking, storytelling, or cinema, Kevin is the kind of person who will gladly share what he knows. He believes opportunities should be shared. He believes people learn by helping each other grow. He has spent years encouraging others to take their first steps into the industry.

Unfortunately, he does not always extend the same kindness to himself.

Despite all the work he has put in, despite all the stories he has written, despite all the people he has helped, Kevin occasionally becomes convinced that he is not talented enough. That he is not ready. That he is somehow falling behind.

This website exists because that assessment appears to be incorrect.

The facts are simple.

Kevin has already accomplished things most people only talk about doing.

He has spent years developing his craft.

He has helped others pursue their own creative ambitions.

He has built stories from nothing but imagination and determination.

And perhaps most importantly, he is closer to achieving his dream than he thinks.

So if Kevin happens to be reading this during one of those moments where he is questioning everything, please remember the evidence.

You are not starting from the beginning.

You are not untalented.

You are not behind.

You are a writer.

You are a director.

You are an overthinker.

And you are much closer than you think.

If you agree, please consider leaving a message of encouragement above. Kevin occasionally requires external assistance in recognizing the obvious.
